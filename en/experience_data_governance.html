Context:

UBEMs are increasingly used for operational, time-sensitive applications (e.g., grid stability and load shifting), which makes dynamic (hourly) calibration and validation essential. Yet, at urban scale, validation data often come from commercially sourced datasets (e.g., utility/DHN meters, platform aggregators) that offer coverage but not necessarily fitness-for-purpose: measurements are frequently sparse, short-term, and operationally incomplete, with substantial missingness and inconsistent data quality. This creates a core controversy for UBEM validation: the most accessible urban-scale datasets are the least reliable for assessing high-resolution predictive accuracy, while common missing-data workarounds (dropping buildings, shortening periods, simple imputation) undermine representativeness and temporal generalisability.
